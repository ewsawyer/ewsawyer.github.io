BOULER BOAT BETTING BATTLE

The best boat-bluffing bouyancy boulder bash in the big blue sea.

Background

Boulder Boat Betting Battle was created for an assignment about affordances in USC's Introduction to Game Design class.

Each design team was given an object, and had to make a game based primarily on the affordances of that object.

We were given a set of plastic boats.

The boats floated easily in water, which was an affordance we all noticed pretty quickly, but I pointed out that the boats had space to put small objects in, and that they would sink if they got too full.

From then on, we were set on making a game about sinking boats.

Challenges

From the start, B.B.B.B. had a small set of rules that really compelled playtesters to keep playing and enjoy it. Our problem was figuring out how to turn these mechanics which people already loved into actual progress towards winning or losing the game.

The main challenge was to figure out how the player should be punished or rewarded for the bets they place during gameplay.

Gameplay

To start, the game box is filled with water.

Then, everyone picks a boat and floats in the water.

All players then grabs a cup and a pile of rocks, and round 1 begins.

In each round, players hide a secret number of rocks under their cup.

Then, one player makes a bid, guessing the total number of rocks under all the players cups.

A bid of "eight" for example, means that that player thinks there are at least eight total rocks under all the cups.

Players go around making bids until someone thinks the last bid was too high.

Once this happens, everyone reveals their rocks, and the total number is counted.

If the last bid was higher than the total...

The last bidder  takes the difference between their bid and the real number, and puts that many rocks in their boat.

If the last bid was lower than the total...

The person who stopped the bid  takes the difference between the last bid and the real number, and puts that many rocks in their boat.

Players are slowly eliminated until only the winner remains!
